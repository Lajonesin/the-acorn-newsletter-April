Hurricane Season & Community Safety

Hurricane season runs June 1 through November 30. As we head into the warmer months, it's a good time to review emergency protocols and what to expect from our community's safety resources.

🚨 In an Emergency

For life-threatening situations, always call 911 first. Police and fire are your first line of response.

For after-hours property emergencies, contact the guard station at 713-785-3135. Guards can relay information to management and assist with access-related issues.

👋 Our Courtesy Staff

You may have noticed the attendants at our entrance gate — and wondered exactly what their role is. They are best described as community courtesy staff, not security guards in the traditional sense. They aren't armed, they don't have law enforcement authority, and they aren't able to physically intervene in disputes or emergencies.

What they do provide is real value: they greet and log visitors, issue guest parking passes, monitor gate access, and serve as a visible presence that deters casual opportunism. They also act as a communication bridge — relaying concerns to management and keeping an eye out for anything worth noting.

We also have cameras at the entrance and surrounding the office area. Full perimeter camera coverage isn't currently feasible given the size of the property, but the entrance is monitored. Ultimately, a safe community depends on all of us — knowing your neighbors, keeping gates closed, and reporting concerns promptly.

✅ What You Can Do

  • Keep all gates closed and self-latching at all times — never prop them open
  • Report any lighting concerns to the office so they can be addressed
  • Be a good neighbor — awareness and communication are our greatest safety tools
